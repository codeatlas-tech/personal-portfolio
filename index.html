About

An education built in the real world

From rural hardware experiments to AI operating systems—driven by science & tech, not theory.

The path I chose

I started building early—hardware, robots, and automation systems instead of waiting for “the right time”.

My early projects ranged from rural tools to civic robots. After 12th, instead of a safe degree path, I chose to learn by doing—launching products, breaking things, and understanding how systems behave in reality.

Some ventures failed, some shipped. All of them pushed me toward one theme: compressing messy operations into reliable AI systems.

How I think

Build > plan. Reality > theory. Science & tech are tools to attack real problems, not just topics to talk about.

What I’m working on

Meson AI Lab, Efficidex, and Default—autonomous operating systems that let small teams operate with the leverage of full departments.
